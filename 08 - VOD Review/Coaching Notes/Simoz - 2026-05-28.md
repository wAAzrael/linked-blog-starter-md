# Coaching Notes — Simoz — 28 May 2026

## Main Review Framework

### Attack Side — T Side

Review:

- **Pathing:** Was my route appropriate for the plan and available information?
- **Fight decision:** Was the duel necessary, supported, and favourable?
- **Utility usage:** Did the utility create space, isolate a fight, or support a teammate?

### Defense Side — CT Side

Review:

- **Position:** Did the position match the round's objective and enemy tendencies?
- **Win conditions:** Did I understand what had to happen for us to win the round?
- **Utility usage:** Did I use utility too early, too late, or without a follow-up?

## Mechanics Framework

Evaluate mechanics through:

- **Movement**
- **Raw aim**
- **Crosshair placement**

> Do not label every lost duel as “bad aim.” Identify which mechanical component actually failed.

## Game-Sense and Problem-Solving Model

Original coaching relationship:

> Game sense → knowledge → problem solving  
> Problem solving → knowledge → game sense

Practical interpretation:

- **Knowledge** gives possible solutions.
- **Problem solving** selects and adapts a solution to the current round.
- Repeated problem solving builds usable **game sense**.
- Game sense is not only memorizing correct plays; it is recognizing and solving changing situations.

## Complete Performance Areas

- Mechanics
- Mentality
- Game sense
- Self-analysis
- Fight decisions
- Confidence

## Fight Creation

A favourable fight can be created through:

1. **Position**
2. **Ability usage**
3. **Crosshair placement**

Before blaming execution, ask whether the fight itself was constructed well.

## Homework

### Ranked Blocks 1–2

- Play two focused ranked blocks.
- After each ranked game, spend **10 timed minutes** reviewing the VOD.
- Use the attack-side, defense-side, mechanics, and fight-creation frameworks above.
- Stop after ten minutes to keep the review focused and repeatable.

### Ranked Blocks 3–4

- Play another two focused ranked blocks.
- Check whether the reviewed behaviours changed.
- Look specifically for active **problem solving**, rather than only repeating memorized plays.
- Improvement is present when decisions adapt to the actual information in the round.
